#Slide: 4
#Header: Introduction to Consumer Behaviour Theory
#Content: Consumer behaviour theory studies how individuals, groups, and organizations make decisions about acquiring and using goods and services.

#Slide: 5
#Header: Factors Influencing Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Consumer behaviour is influenced by various factors such as cultural, social, personal, and psychological factors.

#Slide: 6
#Header: Consumer Decision-Making Process
#Content: The consumer decision-making process includes problem recognition, information search, evaluation of alternatives, purchase decision, and post-purchase evaluation.

#Slide: 7
#Header: Theoretical Models of Consumer Behaviour
#Content: The Fishbein Model, the Theory of Reasoned Action, and the Theory of Planned Behaviour are some of the theoretical models used to explain consumer behaviour.

#Slide: 8
#Header: Perception and Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Perception refers to how individuals interpret and organize the stimuli they encounter. It plays a vital role in consumer decision-making.

#Slide: 9
#Header: Attitudes and Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Attitudes are evaluations that individuals hold about various products, services, or brands. They influence consumer behaviour.

#Slide: 10
#Header: Motivation and Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Motivation is the internal driving force that stimulates individuals to fulfill their needs and desires, impacting their consumer behaviour.

#Slide: 11
#Header: Personality and Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Personality traits influence individuals' preferences and consumption patterns, leading to different consumer behaviours.

#Slide: 12
#Header: Group Influence on Consumer Behaviour
#Content: The opinions, recommendations, and behaviour of reference groups, family, and friends can significantly impact consumer decisions.

#Slide: 13
#Header: Culture and Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Culture encompasses the shared beliefs, values, customs, and behaviours of a group. It influences consumers' product preferences and purchase decisions.

#Slide: 14
#Header: The Role of Marketing in Influencing Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Marketing strategies and tactics aim to influence consumer behaviour through various techniques such as advertising, branding, and product design.

#Slide: 15
#Header: Ethical and Social Responsibility in Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Ethical and socially responsible businesses consider the impact of their products, services, and marketing activities on consumers and society.

#Slide: 16
#Header: Understanding Consumer Behaviour Through Market Research
#Content: Market research involves gathering and analyzing data to understand consumers' preferences, attitudes, and behaviours, helping businesses develop effective strategies.

#Slide: 17
#Header: Case Studies on Consumer Behaviour
#Content: Examining real-world case studies provides practical insights into how consumer behaviour theory applies to different industries and scenarios.

#Slide: 18
#Header: Summary
#Content: In summary, consumer behaviour theory provides a framework to understand how and why consumers make decisions. It considers various factors influencing consumer behaviour and helps businesses develop marketing strategies to meet consumers' needs and desires.

#Slide: END
